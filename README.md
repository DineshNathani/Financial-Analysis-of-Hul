# Financial-Analysis-of-Hul
HUL financial model, DCF valuation, ratio analysis FY2016–2025

About This Project
A comprehensive financial analysis of Hindustan Unilever Limited (HUL) — India's largest FMCG company — covering 10 years of historical performance (FY2016–FY2025) and 5-year forward projections (FY2026–FY2030). The project was built entirely in Excel and covers everything from raw data to a full DCF valuation.

Tools & Techniques Used
Excel Model (7 Sheets)

Data Sheet — Raw financial data sourced from HUL's annual reports (P&L, Balance Sheet, Cash Flow)
3 Statement Model — Fully integrated Income Statement, Balance Sheet, and Cash Flow Statement with historical actuals and future projections
Ratio Analysis — 25+ financial ratios across profitability, solvency, efficiency, liquidity, and market metrics
DCF Valuation — Discounted Cash Flow model using FCFF methodology, WACC built from CAPM, with terminal value and sensitivity analysis
COMP Analysis — Comparable company analysis benchmarking HUL against listed FMCG peers on EV/Revenue, EV/EBITDA, and P/E
Common Size Statement — Vertical analysis of P&L and Balance Sheet to identify structural cost shifts over the decade
Trend Analysis — Revenue trend with YoY growth decomposition and projection assumptions

Financial Concepts Applied

CAPM for Cost of Equity (Rf + β × ERP)
WACC calculation with market-value weights
Free Cash Flow to Firm (FCFF) = NOPAT + D&A − Capex − ΔNWC
Terminal Value using Gordon Growth Model
Negative Cash Conversion Cycle analysis
Ind AS 116 lease capitalisation impact adjustment
Common size and trend analysis for structural insight


🔍 Key Findings
Business Performance

HUL grew revenue from ₹32,186 Cr (FY2016) to ₹63,121 Cr (FY2025), a 10-year CAGR of ~7.8%, but growth has slowed sharply to ~2% post-FY2023 due to rural demand weakness and exhausted pricing power
Despite commodity headwinds in FY2022–23, HUL protected EBITDA margins in the 23–26% band — a sign of strong pricing discipline and operating leverage
Net Profit Margin has remained remarkably stable between 12.9%–16.9% across the entire decade

Balance Sheet & Cash Flow

HUL runs a negative Cash Conversion Cycle of −50.9 days — meaning it collects cash from customers before paying suppliers, effectively financing operations with supplier credit
The company is virtually debt-free (Debt/Equity of 0.033x) with ₹7,554 Cr cash on the Balance Sheet
FY2021 saw a near 9x jump in Net Block purely due to Ind AS 116 adoption — not a real asset acquisition — which distorted ROA, ROE, and asset turnover ratios for subsequent years

Valuation

DCF at a 9.91% WACC and 3.5% terminal growth rate implies a fair value of ₹244/share on historical FCFF — suggesting HUL's current market price of ₹2,376 is pricing in significant future earnings acceleration
At a P/E of 54x, the market is paying a substantial premium for HUL's consistency, brand strength, and defensive characteristics
Peer comparison confirms HUL trades at a well-established premium — justified relative to true FMCG comps like Dabur (~35x) and Marico (~45x), though not vs. non-comparable peers

Risk Flags Identified

Receivables Days rising from 14 days (FY2016) to 22 days (FY2025) — worth monitoring
FY2025 dividend payout (₹12,455 Cr) exceeded net profit (₹10,649 Cr), funded from accumulated reserves — signals parent Unilever's aggressive cash repatriation
Near-term revenue projections assume a sharp rural recovery — a key risk if rural demand remains subdued into FY2027
