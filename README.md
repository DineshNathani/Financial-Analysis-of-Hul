## 🗂️ Project Structure

| Sheet | Contents |
|-------|----------|
| `Data` | Raw financials sourced from HUL annual reports (P&L, Balance Sheet, Cash Flow) |
| `3-Statement Model` | Integrated Income Statement, Balance Sheet & Cash Flow — historical + projected |
| `Ratio Analysis` | 25+ ratios across profitability, solvency, efficiency, liquidity & market metrics |
| `DCF Valuation` | FCFF-based DCF with WACC (CAPM) and Gordon Growth Model terminal value |
| `COMP Analysis` | Peer benchmarking vs. Dabur, Marico on EV/Revenue, EV/EBITDA, P/E |
| `Common Size` | Vertical analysis of P&L and Balance Sheet — decade-long structural shifts |
| `Trend Analysis` | Revenue trend with YoY growth decomposition and projection assumptions |

## 🧠 Financial Concepts Applied

- **CAPM** for Cost of Equity → `Ke = Rf + β × ERP`
- **WACC** with market-value weights
- **FCFF** = `NOPAT + D&A − Capex − ΔNWC`
- **Terminal Value** via Gordon Growth Model
- **Negative Cash Conversion Cycle** analysis
- **Ind AS 116** lease capitalisation impact adjustment
- Common size & trend analysis for structural insight

## 🔍 Key Findings

### 📈 Business Performance
- HUL grew revenue from **₹32,186 Cr (FY2016)** to **₹63,121 Cr (FY2025)** — a 10-year CAGR of ~7.8%
- Growth has slowed to ~2% post-FY2023 due to rural demand weakness and exhausted pricing power
- Despite commodity headwinds in FY2022–23, HUL held **EBITDA margins in the 23–26% band** — a sign of strong operating leverage
- Net Profit Margin remained stable between **12.9%–16.9%** across the entire decade

### 🏦 Balance Sheet & Cash Flow
- HUL runs a **negative Cash Conversion Cycle of −50.9 days** — collecting cash before paying suppliers
- Virtually **debt-free** (D/E of 0.033x) with ₹7,554 Cr cash on the Balance Sheet
- FY2021 saw a ~9x jump in Net Block due to **Ind AS 116 adoption** — not a real asset acquisition — distorting ROA, ROE and asset turnover

### 💰 Valuation
- DCF at **9.91% WACC** and **3.5% terminal growth** implies a fair value of **₹244/share** on historical FCFF
- Current market price of **₹2,376** prices in significant future earnings acceleration
- At **54x P/E**, the market pays a premium for HUL's consistency — justified vs. Dabur (~35x) and Marico (~45x)

### ⚠️ Risk Flags Identified
- Receivables Days rising from **14 days (FY2016) → 22 days (FY2025)** — early warning signal
- FY2025 dividend payout (₹12,455 Cr) **exceeded net profit** (₹10,649 Cr) — funded from reserves, signals aggressive cash repatriation by parent Unilever
- Near-term projections assume a **sharp rural demand recovery** — a key risk if subdued into FY2027
